How to Feed Your Food Obsession in Japan

Photo by Alex Kim

Sushi: A Culinary Artform

Japan's most famous culinary export, sushi, needs no introduction. Delight your taste buds with an array of sushi varieties, from traditional nigiri with fresh fish to creative rolls with unique flavor combinations. Visit Tsukiji Outer Market in Tokyo or local sushiya (sushi restaurants) in Osaka to savor the freshest and finest sushi prepared by skilled chefs.

Ramen: Noodles for the Soul

Embrace the comforting warmth of ramen, Japan's beloved noodle dish. Each region boasts its distinct ramen style, whether it's the rich tonkotsu broth of Fukuoka, the soy-based shoyu ramen of Tokyo, or the miso-infused ramen of Sapporo. Customize your bowl with various toppings and slurp your way to noodle heaven.

Tempura: Crispy Perfection

Indulge in the delicate art of tempura, where lightly battered and perfectly fried seafood, vegetables, and more await. Experience the contrast of crispy exterior and tender interior as you savor tempura at renowned restaurants in Kyoto or the bustling streets of Dotonbori in Osaka.

Matcha: Green Elixir

Immerse yourself in the world of matcha, a vibrant green tea powder celebrated for its health benefits and cultural significance. Participate in traditional tea ceremonies or enjoy modern matcha-infused treats like matcha ice cream or matcha-flavored pastries in Kyoto's charming teahouses.

Izakaya: A Taste of Local Culture

Discover the heart of Japanese social life at izakayas, lively gastropubs where locals gather to unwind and bond over drinks and delicious small plates. Sample a variety of Japanese delicacies, such as yakitori (grilled skewers), takoyaki (octopus balls), and kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) while sipping on sake or shochu.
